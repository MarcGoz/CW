LOWER PRICES, RAISE WAGES

A campaign urging the regime to increase the minimum wage for our people

Posted by KLOD GUMAWA IG 4 ka samen on February 26, 2023

The first half of 2022 saw a real decline in worldwide monthly earnings of 0.9%, according to The Global Wage Report 2022-2023: The Effect of inflation and COVID-19 on wages and buying power.

The COVID-19 crisis, which in many nations had the biggest impact on low-income groups, resulted in considerable salary losses for employees and their families. The cost-of-living crisis comes on top of these wage losses.

It is observed that the increasing inflation around the world has made people, particularly those belonging to the bottom of society, suffer from a lack of financial capacity to sustain their daily needs. Moreover, since the prices of goods and services are still increasing, so does the gap between the rich and poor (those who have less access to opportunities), thus the "income inequality" in the community.

In order to mitigate this global issue, stand with our advocacy in urging the national government to take concrete actions that will elevate the citizens, especially the impoverished and marginalized.
